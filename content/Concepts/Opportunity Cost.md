# Opportunity Cost

> The value of the next-best alternative you give up when you make a choice.

Every decision is not just about what you gain — it's about what you give up. If you spend 2 hours on Project A, those 2 hours can't go to Project B. That's the opportunity cost.

## Why this matters for prioritization

Most people think about the cost of doing something. Opportunity cost forces you to think about the **cost of not doing something else**.

This reframes the question from:
- *"Is this worth doing?"* → *"Is this worth doing more than everything else I could do instead?"*

## Cross-domain connections

### In business
Customer acquisition costs must be weighed against retention costs. The "opportunity" is not just revenue from a new customer — it's the lost focus from the customer you didn't spend time retaining. See: [[Concepts/Customer Retention vs Acquisition]]

### In real estate
Every dollar in a lease renewal deal is one not deployed in a new acquisition. Property managers face this constantly: is renewing this tenant worth more than the vacancy risk of finding a better one? This mirrors the thinking in subscription businesses.

### In personal decisions
Choosing to stay in a comfortable situation has an opportunity cost too — the growth, income, or experience you'd have gained by taking the riskier path.
