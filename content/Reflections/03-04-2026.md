## Finding opportunities
I think a lot of the times I think that opportunities have to fall into my lap, or I have to be lucky, or I have to be in the right place at the right time. 
Or even that I have to know the right people. Sometimes that is true, but I was reflecting on what I learned in The Compound Effect: that you find what you're looking for.
For example, you start car shopping. You decide that you want a white Toyota RAV4. All of a sudden, you see these cars everywhere. 
On the streets and in parking lots, you notice them; even your neighbor down the street has one. 
These cars have always been there, you just weren't consciously noticing them. You find what you're looking for. 
You think opportunities are scarce, but in reality, they are everywhere. 
What you focus on and want is what you get out, and you create your own luck.

## How do I apply this?
- Instead of ignoring professionals, go talk to them, introduce yourself, or message them on LinkedIn and thank them.
- Instead of just driving around, notice land and real estate deals to start understanding the market.
- Instead of ignoring marketing, notice the different ads on Instagram to see which companies have good sales.
- Instead of wondering why a certain stock is up, start following the companies and their news.
